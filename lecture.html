Building Proto Personas

Personas are created in order to represent the type of user of our product.

We start by brainstorming assumptions about this personas trying to figure out our target audience: which students are interested in learning online about arts.

continuing our group discussion and voting - we arrive to 2 main types of targets that will represent at this stage our proto personas:

people living in places with no diverse education

Laura,

35 years old
Graphic Designer
Amarante, Portugal

habits

uses WhatsApp frequently; likes to travel; uses public transportation; shops online; follows her favorite artists on Instagram

needs

wants to change her field of work; wants a job in the visual arts; have a better-paid job; would like to find a community of people with the same interests and goals;

obstacles

her education level prevents her from getting a better job; doesn't have educational options near home; her schedule availability depends on family commitments; doesn't have driver's license

people trained in non-artistic areas

Antonio,

42 years old
Biologist
Braga, Portugal

habits

likes to cook to his family on Sundays; grows his own organic fruits and vegetables; watches YouTube tutorials on food photography and image editing; cares about sustainability

needs

learn to cook without following recipes; avoid food waste; learn more about photography; find an organic farming community

obstacles

where the teaching offer is very conventional; it needs to look for new recipes on a regular basis; recipes with difficult ingredients to find; only has access to intensive gastronomy courses - is looking for something easier and practical

User Research Strategy_ Interview

guidelines | questions | answers |analyses

in order to deepen our investigation, we chose the interview method as research.

the main goal of our interviews is to understans how the process of choosing a course works - what are the criteria used by our users. understans their experience in inline learning

what kind of courses can lead to a better job

we try to investigate what the competitors have to offer and if they respond to our users needs - users/ personas we have in mind

COMPETITORS ANALYSES

compairing the 3 most used online learning platforms

Udemy's main message is presenting a global catalog with the latest skills and topics - to it's target audience: people and organizations. Using the key words: adapt | change | thrive. Also demonstrates some of its data: 77M Learners | 75 Languages | 80K Instructors | 250K Courses 17K Enterprise customers | 1B+ Course enrollment

Coursera directs its message to communities around the world, using the key sentences "learning is the source of human progress" and "learning is a right, not a privilege". Has the strategy to "partner with the best institutions to bring the best learning to every corner of the world".

Domestika speaks directly to its audience - the creative community. Uses as key words: create | share | learn. Brings the best creatice professionals to share their knowledge in courses carefully produced in-house. Produces online courses to "anyone interested in unleashing their creative potencial and connecting with like-minded creatives from around the world".
